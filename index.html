The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation's trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus, Nobel Peace Prize laureate and founder of the Grameen Bank, introduced the "Three Zeros" theory in his book A World of Three Zeros. This visionary framework aims to achieve:1.Zero Poverty:By promoting microcredit and social business initiatives, Dr. Yunus envisions a world where poverty is eradicated, empowering individuals to become self-sufficient. 2.Zero Unemployment: Encouraging entrepreneurship, especially among the youth, to create job opportunities and transform unemployed individuals into job creators. 3.Zero Net Carbon Emissions:Advocating for environmentally sustainable practices to combat climate change and ensure a healthier planet for future generations.These goals align with the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and have been recognized as catalysts for achieving them.

Date: 15/01/2025 | Time: 10:25 PM

Bangladesh has made significant strides in space technology with the launch of its first communication satellite, Bangabandhu-1. Launched on May 11, 2018, from NASA's Kennedy Space Center in Florida aboard SpaceX's Falcon 9 rocket, the satellite was successfully deployed into its designated orbital slot at 119.1 degrees East.This milestone positions Bangladesh as the 57th nation to operate a geostationary communication satellite, marking its entry into the global space community.

The Bangabandhu-1 satellite is equipped with 40 transponders—26 Ku-band and 14 C-band—to provide comprehensive telecommunications coverage across Bangladesh and neighboring regions. Its services are expected to enhance Direct-to-Home (DTH) television, internet connectivity, and support applications like e-learning, telemedicine, and disaster recovery
